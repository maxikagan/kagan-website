Max Kagan

Postdoctoral Research Scholar in Management
Columbia Business School

I study how political partisanship and ideology affect business performance using quantitative empirical techniques, including experiments and analysis of large-scale administrative and commercial data.

Much of my research uses voter file data to measure employees' partisanship and understand how this affects organizational outcomes.

I received my Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley. Prior to graduate school, I worked at McKinsey & Company in Dubai and New York, in private equity focused on fintech and business services, and at the Good Jobs Institute.
